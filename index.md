<header class="home-header">
  <h1 class="home-title">If You Can't Beat 'Em</h1>
  <p class="home-tagline">Legacy media figures in the podsphere — and what their reluctant arrival tells us about the collapse of broadcast infrastructure.</p>
</header>

This site collects the academic and public-facing writing on a generative case for studying the structural transformation of American media: the migration of legacy broadcast figures — anchors, correspondents, editors — into the podsphere and onto streaming platforms. The case is not an adaptation story. It is a divestiture story. When Brian Williams opens his Netflix podcast with a joke about every American having their own show by 2030, the sarcasm is a tell: the migration is not voluntary. The infrastructure that once made broadcast journalism the dominant production mode of political communication is contracting, and the figures it shaped are following their audiences into formats they did not build and do not fully control. This package examines that migration through the lenses of hybrid media systems theory, media ecology, and institutional adaptation — and asks what the next decade of American journalism infrastructure looks like when the last analog holdouts have gone.
